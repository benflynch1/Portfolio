01

About
the project

WONE is a workplace wellbeing platform that uses biometric data from wearables to give employees and organisations real-time stress intelligence — grounded in the latest clinical research. The brief was to evolve the app to support a new, validated biomarker: the gold standard for stress measurement.

02

Conducting a
Design Audit

In order to understand the challenges ahead and areas of friction in current product, a design audit of the current product was proposed by the CEO within month 1. Typically, some guardrails would be put in place to focus on specific flows, accessibility, a specific platform or general UX/UI suggestions. The request from leadership team was to audit everything — which required collaborating with product and engineering to quickly understand the product.

03

Ways of
Working

Leadership team requested design audit improvements implemented, alongside BAO product tasks in addition to vision work (a design prototype re-imagining the product) within 3 months. It became an in-perfect process but one where BAO tasks took priority to improve key metrics, design audit updates were implemented adhoc or when engineers were light on tasks between sprints, and vision work turned into an 8 week sprint outside of product tasks.

04

Making content
easier to discover

One of the key findings from the design audit was that Expert Sessions on the platform were suffering from a series of issues. Live Sessions, On-Demand Sessions and Articles were each split into separate pages, leading to abandonment and drop off. They also lacked the ability to filter, search and find newly released or trending sessions.

Our redesign brought these all into the same tab, contained in a nested structure, reducing clicks and friction throughout the process that prevented customers finding the sessions they wanted. Quick action buttons were also introduced on desktop to start or save a session without having to dive back and forth, whilst the Session pages themselves were redesigned to promote practical information about the sessions, practitioners and more.

05

Introducing
Ori

Ori was born from the vision work conducted in Summer 2025. The hypothesis from leadership team was that an AI stress coach could layer in a personalised approach to stress management within the app. The work involved partnering with a Chief Technology Officer and Chief Scientific Officer to establish guardrails and safety parameters within the existing UX of the app.

06

Establishing an
AI design workflow

Alongside shipping features, I established a new design process that used AI tooling for research synthesis, copy iteration and rapid prototyping. Time from raw insight to validated prototype fell by roughly half, and the workflow has since been adopted as the default way of working across the wider product team.
