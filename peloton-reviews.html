All work

Redesigning the entire reviews ecosystem on onepeloton.com — including a Q&A pilot — to build trust through honest, useful customer feedback.

Client
Peloton
Sector
Fitness · Retail
Tags
eCommerce Reviews UGC Pilot
Role
Product Designer
Objective

Increase trust in Peloton products through advanced Reviews, Q&A and user-generated content.

Market

Prospect customers and existing members on onepeloton.com.

Learnings to uncover

A feature with no prior UX research, an unmaintained module, and a complex eCommerce roadmap to navigate.

"I want to read honest and real customer feedback on the utility and how effective the item is. I want to know is it worth it and what are some of the pros and cons so I can better weigh my decision."

UXR Spotlight — Customer voice

01

The
Reviews module

We approached redesigning the product-page module in two phases. First, a quick A/B test to prove customers wanted to read reviews — a dynamic module with a clear path to all product reviews. Second, a more ambitious vision concept put into user testing to push how members and prospects could engage with reviews directly on the product page.

02

The
Reviews page

We launched an MVP redesign of the Reviews page to give the team time to investigate advanced filtering, search, and the content being submitted. Alongside the visual refresh, we fixed a backlog of bugs from the original page — including duplicate review pages for hardware sold in multiple locales.

03

A
Q&A pilot

Research showed prospects often struggled to fully understand how Peloton products would fit into their lives — delivery, set-up, space, weight, noise, spec requirements. The Q&A workstream introduced a way for prospects to ask members directly, building trust around the broader Peloton experience and incrementally lifting hardware conversion.

04

A better
Write a Review flow

Members told us they wanted to leave comprehensive reviews but were missing the prompts — emails were going out 90 days after purchase. We changed the trigger logic and redesigned the questionnaire so members could give richer feedback, and prospects could learn more about the lived Peloton experience.
