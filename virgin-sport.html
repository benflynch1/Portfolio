Designing the digital home for Virgin's first ever sport venture — a festival format that put community celebration alongside competition.

Client
Virgin Sport
Sector
Fitness · Events
Tags
Brand Launch Design System Events Consumer App
Role
Product Designer, AKQA London
Brand challenge

Launch a credible new entrant under the Virgin name in a crowded fitness and events market.

Approach

A flexible component library on the website paired with a brand playbook, designed to scale from digital to print and event environments.

Outcome

Live across three flagship UK events, a registration and race-day experience that supported tens of thousands of participants.

01

Building a
digital home

Virgin Sport's website needed to do three things at once: drive ticket sales for the flagship Hackney Festival of Sport, communicate the broader vision of an inclusive sport brand, and act as a content engine for the run-up to each event.

I worked on the page narrative, breakpoint behaviour, and how the homepage flexed across event seasons. The design language took its cue from event environments — bold typography, photography-led storytelling, and energetic colour blocks anchored by a system that stayed calm enough to support clear conversion paths.

02

A library of
modular components

Working alongside the wider team, I helped design and refine a set of reusable modules — race app prompts, event class tiles, CTA blocks, festival schedule planners, race pack confirmations and more — each tested across breakpoints and content states.

The system gave the marketing and content teams a kit they could compose with autonomously, and reduced the design and engineering cost of every new event going live.

03

Defining the brand
at scale

Alongside the website, I co-authored a brand playbook with designers across AKQA — a single source of truth for partner agencies, photographers, signage producers and the in-house team.

The playbook codified the visual identity, typographic hierarchy and photographic direction so the experience felt coherent whether someone was scrolling the homepage, picking up a race pack on the morning of the event, or watching the Hackney Festival from the sidelines.
