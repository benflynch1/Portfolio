Designing a real-time travel discovery experience powered by local social content and interactive mapping.

Client
Twitter × Visit England
Sector
Travel · Social
Tags
Microsite Travel Brand Partnership Real-time Data
Role
Product Design Lead
Objective

Help visitors to the UK discover real-time travel tips, surfaced through an interactive map of Visit England local offices.

Market

International tourists planning a UK trip, and Visit England local-office accounts on Twitter as content sources.

Approach

A bold, brand-led microsite with two entry points — search by place or by activity — funnelling each into a Twitter-fed map view.

01

UX —
two ways in

For anyone visiting the UK, the microsite was built around a simple flow with two entry points: search by places you'd like to visit, or by activities you'd like to do. Both paths arrived at the same map view — populated with Tweets from Visit England local accounts — giving users a visual, real-time list of things to do nearby.

02

UI —
brand-led, photography-first

The interface used Visit England's brand system — typography, colour palette and photography — to create a bold, clean experience that put imagery of England at the centre of every screen. Twitter's visual language sat alongside it, lightly applied so the content felt curated rather than feed-driven.

03

The
final experience

A responsive microsite that paired Visit England's brand expression with Twitter's real-time content layer — built to feel premium on the home page and pragmatic in the map view, where utility had to lead.
